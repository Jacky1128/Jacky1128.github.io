Short Bio
Zedong Wang (Jacky) is currently a first-year CSE PhD student at The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST), advised by Prof. Dan Xu. Previously, he was a visiting student at Westlake University, advised by Chair Prof. Stan Z. Li. He received his bachelor's degree in Electronics and Information Engineering from Huazhong University of Science and Technology (HUST) in 2023, where he was fortunately advised by Prof. Xinggang Wang.

He has been recognized as an Outstanding Reviewer at several prestigious AI/CV conferences, such as ECCV'24, MM'24, and BMVC'24. His research centers on computer vision and multi-task learning, with a specific focus on efficient network architectures, learning paradigms, and optimization techniques, as well as their applications in autonomous driving and more.

Research Interests

 Currently, I focus mostly on Computer Vision and Efficient Multi-task Learning, including (but not limited to):
  • Data-efficient Learning: Mixup Augmentation [OpenMixup, SAMix], Semi-supervised Learning [SemiReward].
  • Efficient Network Architectures: Vision Backbones [MogaNet], Multi-task Architectures [MergeVQ].
  • Optimization Techniques: Optimizers [SEMA, BOCB], Multi-task Optimization.
  • Downstream Applications: End-to-End Autonomous Driving.
